# Agentic Support Layer

This document captures how Codex or another agentic harness can help Jack
accelerate his art business while keeping the work low-pressure, accountable,
and aligned with both the summer 2026 revenue milestone and Jack's long-term goal
of using his art and creativity as the foundation for his career.

## Operating Principle

The agent should not tell Jack what to draw or turn art into homework. The agent
should help preserve, package, organize, and sell the work Jack creates when he
is inspired.

Short-term agent work should help Jack make money this summer. Long-term agent
work should also build assets and habits that support future creative income:
portfolio, catalog, audience, product experiments, sales records, decision
history, and confidence using art as a career path.

The best agent behavior is:

- Notice finished work.
- Turn finished work into business assets.
- Keep plans small and completable.
- Maintain durable records in this repo.
- Keep Linear aligned with small next actions.
- Reduce administrative friction for Jack and his parent/guardian.

## Primary Agent Role

### Catalog and Momentum Assistant

This should be the first agent role to build around.

Responsibilities:

- Help turn finished artwork into catalog entries.
- Suggest titles, descriptions, metadata, and product possibilities.
- Identify which pieces might work as originals, prints, stickers, cards, zines,
  digital products, or future drops.
- Propose the smallest useful next business action.
- Keep track of what has been cataloged, photographed, priced, shared, sold, or
  paused.

This role should support Jack's creative rhythm instead of imposing a production
schedule.

## Useful Agent Loops

### Creative Loop

Jack creates art when inspired. After a piece exists, the agent helps convert it
into business-ready context.

Flow:

1. Jack finishes or finds a piece.
2. Agent helps catalog it.
3. Agent suggests product paths.
4. Jack approves or ignores suggestions.
5. Approved next actions become small tasks.

Possible agent outputs:

- Title options.
- Plain-language description.
- Medium, dimensions, date, and status fields.
- Price range suggestion.
- Productization suggestion.
- Drop or collection fit.

### Business Loop

The agent keeps business work moving through tiny, finishable tasks.

Flow:

1. Agent proposes a small set of next actions.
2. Approved tasks go into Linear.
3. Tasks are kept short, concrete, and verifiable.
4. Completed work is reflected back into this repo.

Good tasks:

- Photograph 3 finished pieces.
- Pick 1 piece to test as a print.
- Draft a short available-work description.
- Add prices to 5 catalog items.
- Ask 3 trusted people which piece they would buy.

Avoid tasks like:

- Build the whole business.
- Launch full store.
- Grow audience.
- Make a new collection by a deadline.

### Sales Loop

The agent helps package and share finished available work.

Flow:

1. Finished work is cataloged.
2. A piece or small group of pieces is selected for a sales test.
3. Agent drafts copy, page text, captions, or outreach.
4. Jack or parent/guardian decides what to share.
5. Responses, sales, and feedback are logged.
6. Agent suggests the next smallest experiment.

## Agent Functions

### Art Catalog Manager

Maintain a structured inventory of Jack's finished work.

Possible fields:

- Title
- Date created
- Medium
- Dimensions
- Status
- Original price
- Print-ready status
- Product ideas
- Notes
- Image/file path

### Tiny Task Planner

Break every project into small tasks that can be completed quickly. Smaller and
more completable is better than long-running work.

Each task should have:

- One clear action.
- One owner: Jack, parent/guardian, Codex, or other agent.
- A visible done condition.
- A connection to the primary goal.

### Weekly Accountability Check-In

A recurring check-in should be lightweight and nonjudgmental.

Questions:

- What did Jack make this week?
- Are any finished pieces ready to catalog?
- Did anything get shared, sold, or discussed?
- Is anything blocked or annoying?
- What is the smallest next business step?

Outputs:

- Brief weekly note in this repo.
- Updated Linear tasks if needed.
- 1-3 suggested next actions.

### Research Agent

Research should answer practical questions that reduce uncertainty.

Examples:

- Print-on-demand vs local print shop options.
- Sticker vendors.
- Pricing ranges for youth/student art.
- Artist shop examples.
- Local San Francisco opportunities that do not require Jack to travel often.
- Platform rules for minors, payments, shipping, and account ownership.

### Shop and Portfolio Builder

Codex can help create and maintain:

- Available works page.
- Artist bio.
- Product pages.
- Sold-work archive.
- Mailing list signup copy.
- Private preview page for friends/family.

### Productization Agent

For each finished piece, suggest possible product forms:

- Original only.
- Fine art print.
- Mini print.
- Sticker.
- Postcard.
- Greeting card.
- Zine page.
- Digital wallpaper.
- Apparel graphic.
- Small themed collection/drop.

### Content Assistant

The agent can turn available art and casual process notes into:

- Instagram captions.
- Product descriptions.
- Newsletter blurbs.
- Short process writeups.
- Available-work announcements.

Avoid rigid posting schedules unless Jack explicitly wants one.

### Linear Operator

Linear is the operating system for projects and tasks, but tasks should stay
small.

Agent responsibilities:

- Create approved projects and tasks in the Jack's Art Business initiative.
- Keep tasks concrete and quick to finish.
- Mark stale or blocked work clearly.
- Keep this repo and Linear aligned.

### Decision Journal

The agent should preserve business decisions in this repo so future work does not
need to rediscover the same context.

Record:

- Models considered.
- What Jack liked or disliked.
- Pricing decisions.
- Buyer/supporter feedback.
- What sold.
- What felt sustainable.
- What should not be repeated.

### Parent/Guardian Operations Assistant

Because Jack is 16, a parent or guardian may need to own or supervise storefront,
payment, tax, and shipping accounts.

Agent support can include:

- Setup checklists.
- Platform comparisons.
- Minor-policy notes.
- Shipping workflow.
- Basic expense/revenue tracking.
- Draft messages and operational docs.

## Recommended First Agentic Project

Project: Build Jack's Available Art Catalog

Agent-owned tasks:

- Draft the catalog schema.
- Create starter data files or docs.
- Draft metadata for the first finished pieces.
- Suggest price ranges and product paths.
- Create a simple dashboard view once data exists.

Jack-owned tasks:

- Pick the first pieces to include.
- Approve titles/descriptions/prices.
- Decide which pieces feel available to sell.

Parent/guardian-owned tasks:

- Help with account/payment decisions.
- Review any public-facing sales setup.
- Handle platform ownership where required.

## Success Criteria

The agentic support layer is working if:

- Finished art becomes cataloged business assets.
- Jack has fewer administrative steps between creating and selling.
- Linear contains small, finishable tasks.
- This repo accumulates useful memory.
- The business moves forward without forcing Jack into deadline-heavy creative
  work.
